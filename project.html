About our problem

NASA needs to collect samples of cosmic dust. This creates the need to develop a cosmic dust collection system that can be mounted to the primary payload of a high-altitude balloon. This system will be composed of two subsystems, the rotating arm subsystem and the motor control subsystem. The rotating arm subsystem needs to collect samples of cosmic dust and protect these samples until the high-altitude balloon has descended and landed.

Cosmic Dust Collector

NASA will utilize the Cosmic Dust Collector System, including KAMP Systemsâ€™ rotating arm subsystem, to take samples of cosmic dust. The user will interact with the rotating arm subsystem through the motor control subsystem. The motor control subsystem will spin the rotating arm subsystem at 100 RPM during mission mode. Dust collection will only occur at the user-defined altitude. The system is scheduled to have an operational duration of 30 days and a mission duration of up to 10 days. The rotating arm subsystem will be mounted to the motor control subsystem, which will be mounted to the primary payload, prior to launch. NASA will be launching the high altitude balloon in April 2021 with the primary payload. The cosmic dust collected by this module will be used for analysis by NASA scientists and engineers.
